Brownfield land register

The Council is responsible for preparing and maintaining a register of previously developed land suitable for residential development under the Town and Country Planning (Brownfield Land Register) Regulations 2017 (the Regulations), which came into force on 16 April 2017.

The Register will provide up-to-date and consistent information on sites that the Local Planning Authority considers to be appropriate for residential development, with the intention of helping house builders identify suitable sites quickly and speeding up the development of new homes. For the purposes of the Register, brownfield or previously developed land has the same definition of Previously Developed Land as set out in Annex 2 of the National Planning Policy Framework.

The Register is in two parts:

  • Part 1 - includes all sites which are ‘suitable’, ‘available’, and ‘achievable’ for residential development, which could be delivered within 15 years; and
  • Part 2 - is a subset of Part 1 and comprises only those sites that the Local Planning Authority has decided would be suitable for grant of ‘permission in principle’ for residential development. This is a new form of planning permission to specifically facilitate the redevelopment of brownfield sites.

[Definitions of ‘suitable’, ‘available’ and ‘achievable’ are included in Regulation 4 of the Regulations]

The Council may choose to include sites within Part 2 of the Register – this would grant such sites ‘Permission in Principle’ for residential development. The Council will have the final say on which sites to include in the Register and which sites will have permission in principle. At this stage the Council is not proposing to include any sites within Part 2 of the Register. A report setting out the Council’s approach to the Register is available here. 

If you would like the Council to consider a site for inclusion in the next version of the Register, please send details of the site location (including an outline plan) and any details of the site’s availability and suitability for residential development, by email to localplanlocalplan@ryedale.gov.uk?subject=Brownfield%20Sites

Please email any queries about brownfield registers and permission in principle to the Department of Communities and Local Governmentbrownfieldregistersandpermissioninprinciple@communities.gsi.gov.uk.
