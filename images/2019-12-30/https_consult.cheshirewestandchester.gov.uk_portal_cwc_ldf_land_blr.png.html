Brownfield Land Register

What is a brownfield land register?

Local planning authorities are required to prepare and maintain a register of previously developed land: a Brownfield Land Register (BLR). The purpose of the brownfield land register is to provide up-to-date and consistent information, in a standardised way across all local planning authorities in England, for appropriate brownfield sites that are considered suitable and available for housing-led development.

There are two parts to a brownfield land register:

  • Part 1: required (by Regulation 3 of Brownfield Land Register Regulations 2017) by all relevant local planning authorities, a list of brownfield sites appropriate for residential development where the relevant criteria are met; and
  • Part 2: a subset of Part 1, identifies appropriate brownfield sites that have been granted permission in principle for residential development. Local planning authorities are able to trigger a grant of permission in principle for residential development for sites in the register where the required procedures are followed. At the current time, the Council does not propose to grant permission in principle on sites, however this will be reviewed annually alongside the Part 1 register.

For a site to be considered for inclusion on the Part 1 register, it must:

  • be wholly or partially previously developed land (brownfield);
  • have a site area > 0.25 hectares or a capacity of five or more dwellings;
  • be suitable with regard to local and national planning policy - a site must be located within or adjacent to an identified main urban area, key or local service centre as set out in the Local Plan (Part One) and publication draft Local Plan (Part Two);
  • be available for development - the owner has expressed an intent to sell; a developer has expressed an intent to develop; or there are no known ownership issues /legal impediments to development; and
  • be achievable for residential development within 15 years; OR
  • be a previously developed site with an extant planning permission for residential development.

The Town and Country Planning (Brownfield Land Register) Regulations 2017 set out the meaning of suitable, available and achievable in Regulation 4 (2), for the purpose of preparing the register, which can be read at http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2017/403/regulation/4/made

The register is based on the Housing and Economic Land Availability Assessment (2017) and the Housing Land Monitor 2016/2017 and has a base date of 1 April 2017. Sites on the monitor may expire before the BLR is next updated and these sites will be reviewed at the appropriate time and may be removed from the register where they no longer meet the requirements set out above. It is also acknowledged that sites with planning permission may subsequently have commenced development and are therefore no longer available. Additional information may become available, or planning policy may change the status of a site for example land allocations for alternative uses. This information will be updated as part of the required annual review. 

Viewing the Brownfield Land Register

To view the Part 1 register and accompanying location maps please click on the supporting documents tab below. Here you will be able to access the following:

The Council's register has been prepared in accordance with Regulations 2017, the Planning Practice Guidance, and the data standards; and has been published in the required .csv (Excel) format.

Information on individual sites relates to the suitability of the site for housing development; it does not provide specific details of ownership, the intention of the owners, or valuation and sale price information. The register should not be viewed as a single comprehensive source of housing development sites, and is subject to change / amendments as well as annual updates in line with the regulations and guidance.

What happens next?

The Council are required to review and update the Part 1 register on an annual basis. As the register includes information from the annual housing land monitor (HLM), it is anticipated that the register will be reviewed accordingly following the publication of the latest HLM report.

The first iteration of the Part 1 register will not be subject to a call for sites as it uses up to date information from the Housing and Economic Land Availaibility Assessment 2017, which was subject to detailed consultation and a call for sites exercise. However, the Council is happy to receive new submissions of appropriate brownfield sites that meet the requirements in the planning practice guidance (summarised above). If you would like to submit a new site for consideration, or provide up to date information on sites included in the register please email: planningpolicy@cheshirewestandchester.gov.uk
