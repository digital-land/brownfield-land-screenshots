Brownfield Land Register

We are required by Government to produce a Brownfield Land Register (BLR). The purpose of the register is to identify brownfield sites that are suitable for housing or housing-led mixed use development. Brownfield land describes land which has been previously developed. Sites within the Brownfield Land Register should meet the Government definition in their entirety.

The Brownfield Land Register is in two parts:

Part 1: identifies suitable brownfield sites that can accommodate five dwellings or more and are available and achievable. Inclusion on this list does not necessarily mean that the site would be granted planning permission.

Part 2: is a list of sites that, from Part 1, that have been granted 'Permission in Principle'.

'Permission in Principle' establishes whether a site is suitable in-principle for residential development, and then allows for "technical detail consent" when the detailed development proposals are assessed. Entering suitable sites in Part 2 of the brownfield land register will trigger a grant of 'permission in principle'.

The Council does not have any sites on Part 2 of the Brownfield Land Register. If and when any sites are to be included on part 2 of the register, to grant permission in principle, appropriate consultation will take place.

The Brownfield Land Register will be reviewed annually to assess the status of sites within the City.
