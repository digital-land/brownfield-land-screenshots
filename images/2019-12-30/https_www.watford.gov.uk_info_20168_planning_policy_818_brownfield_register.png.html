Brownfield Register

You are here:
  1. Home
  2. Planning and building control
  3. Planning policy
  4. Brownfield Register

The Town and Country Planning (Register of Previously Developed Land) Regulations 2017 require councils to produce a Brownfield Register.

The Government wants 90% of suitable brownfield sites to have planning permission for housing by 2020. It is part of the Government’s aim to boost the supply of housing land and to increase the rate new homes are being built.

Sites on the Register had to meet a certain set of criteria, including:

  • be previously developed land;

  • capable of supporting development of 5 or more dwellings or be at least 0.25 hectares in size;

  • suitable for residential use;

  • free from any constraints that cannot be mitigated; and

  • deliverable within 5 years.

If you would like to view the sites included on the Brownfield Register (Dec 2018) it is available in two formats:

  • List of brownfield sites

  • Map of brownfield sites (below)

The Brownfield Register will be updated annually.
