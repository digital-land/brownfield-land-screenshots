Housing Land Supply

We need to maintain a supply of land for housing to ensure homes can be provided for the growing number of people living in the area. The Strategic Housing Land Availability Assessment (SHLAA) lists all the sites that have planning permission or which are allocated for housing in Walsall’s Unitary Development Plan (UDP) or the Site Allocation Document (SAD). It also identifies other sites that we consider have potential for housing.

National planning policy requires local planning authorities to identify and update annually a supply of specific deliverable sites sufficient to provide five years’ worth of housing against their housing requirements. The SHLAA explains what these requirements are and how they will be met by the sites listed.

Much of our housing supply comes from the redevelopment of previously developed land (brownfield sites). The Town and Country Planning (Brownfield Land Register) Regulations 2017 require local authorities to prepare and maintain registers of brownfield land that is suitable for residential development. Walsall’s Brownfield Land Register comprises sites in the SHLAA that meet the relevant criteria for inclusion.
